Alexandre Goupy

PhD Student, ENS Paris-Saclay & CEA

Presentation

I started my PhD on January 2017 at the CMLA (Center for Mathematics and their Applications at ENS Paris-Saclay) and CEA (Atomic Energy and Alternative Energies Commission) under the supervision of Christophe Millet and Didier Lucor.

I am working on wave propagation through random media, using uncertainty quantification techniques to assess numerically the impact of perturbation of the medium on the wave propagation. I am working more specifically on infrasound propagation in the atmosphere.
From a numerical point of view I am coupling deterministic techniques to solve the propagation with stochastic methods to deal with uncertainties.


Keywords

Acoustic propagation - Spectral methods - Uncertainty Quantification - gPC decomposition
